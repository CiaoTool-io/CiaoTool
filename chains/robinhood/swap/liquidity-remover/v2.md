## What Is CiaoTool Robinhood Chain Remove V2 Liquidity?

<figure><picture><source srcset="../../../../.gitbook/assets/ScreenShot_2026-07-22_162317_414.png" media="(prefers-color-scheme: dark)"><img src="../../../../.gitbook/assets/image (1098).png" alt="CiaoTool Robinhood Chain Toolkit about Remove V2 Liquidity Tool page"></picture><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

Removing V2 liquidity means redeeming your Uniswap V2 LP Tokens for your proportional share of the two assets held by the liquidity pool.

When liquidity is added to a V2 pool, the provider receives LP Tokens representing their ownership percentage. During removal, the selected LP Tokens are returned to the protocol and burned. The corresponding underlying assets are then transferred to the configured recipient.

## FAQs

<details>

<summary><strong>What do I receive after removing V2 liquidity?</strong></summary>

You normally receive both assets in the liquidity pool according to your redeemed pool share and the pool’s reserve ratio at execution time.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Can I remove only part of my liquidity?</strong></summary>

Yes. You can select a partial percentage and retain the remaining LP Tokens in your wallet.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Why is the received amount different from the amount originally deposited?</strong></summary>

The pool ratio changes as users trade. Your LP Tokens represent a share of the pool’s current reserves rather than a claim on the exact quantities originally deposited.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Are accumulated trading fees included?</strong></summary>

In standard V2 pools, trading fees are generally added to the pool reserves. LP holders receive their proportional share of the current reserves when removing liquidity, so their position value may reflect accumulated fees.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Does removing liquidity change the token price?</strong></summary>

In a standard V2 pool, liquidity is removed proportionally from both reserves. This normally does not intentionally change the pool’s reserve ratio or spot price at the moment of removal.

However, it reduces liquidity depth. A shallower pool may experience greater price impact and volatility from subsequent trades.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>How does CiaoTool protect the imported private key?</strong></summary>

The private key is processed locally in the browser and is not uploaded, transmitted, stored, logged, or written to Local Storage. Imported private-key data is cleared when the page is closed or refreshed.

</details>
