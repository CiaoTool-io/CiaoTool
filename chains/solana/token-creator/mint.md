## What Is Token Minting?

Token minting relies on the **Mint Authority**. Only the wallet holding this authority can create additional tokens. Once the Mint Authority is revoked, the total token supply becomes permanently fixed and no further minting can occur.

**Common Use Cases:**

* **Increase Supply**: Mint additional tokens based on project or market needs
* **Liquidity Provision**: Add more tokens to liquidity pools
* **Rewards & Airdrops**: Distribute tokens for incentives and community programs
* **Supply Management**: Adjust circulating supply as part of token strategies
* **Reserved Allocation**: Release tokens in planned stages over time

## How to Mint Additional Tokens?

1. **Connect Wallet**

* Click \[Connect Wallet] in the top right corner, select your wallet address, and approve the authorization.
* Once the wallet address is displayed in the top right corner of the page, it indicates that the wallet has been successfully connected.

2. **Select Token**

<figure><img src="../../../.gitbook/assets/image (769).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

* Paste the token address or select a token within your wallet from the drop-down menu.
* If you possess the mint authority and it has not been renounced, the mint button will be displayed; otherwise, it will show "Renounced".
* You must connect the wallet that holds the ownership/authority of the token; otherwise, it will show "Not Owned".

**3. Set Mint Quantity**

* Input the amount to be minted; theoretically, there is no limit on the quantity.

#### ❓ Frequently Asked Questions <a href="#frequently-asked-questions" id="frequently-asked-questions"></a>

**1. Can token permissions be restored after revocation?**

* **Answer:** No. Once permissions are revoked, the action is **permanent and cannot be undone**.

**2. Why should I revoke token permissions?**

* **Answer:** Revoking permissions **increases project transparency**, strengthens community trust, and attracts more investors.

**3. Is the revocation process complicated?**

* **Answer:** Not at all. With **CiaoTool**, it’s a **one-click operation** — no coding required.

**4. Which permissions are most important to revoke?**

* **Answer:** Typically, the **Mint** (minting) and **Freeze** (account freezing) permissions are key to ensuring supply integrity and operational safety.

**5. Will revoking permissions affect token trading?**

* **Answer:** No. **Regular transfers and trading** of the token will remain unaffected.
